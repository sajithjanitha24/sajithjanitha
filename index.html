I am an enthusiastic and dedicated engineering graduate with a strong background in electrical and electronic systems, passionate about using technology to solve real-world problems. I specialize in creating innovative solutions for environmental and wildlife conservation challenges, particularly through the use of sensors, automation, and AI. I thrive in multidisciplinary teams, continuously expanding my skills, and applying theoretical knowledge in practical, hands-on projects. My goal is to leverage my expertise to develop sustainable solutions that address pressing societal issues, such as wildlife protection and infrastructure safety.

One of my major projects focuses on developing an AI-assisted elephant detection system using seismic vibration sensors to prevent train-elephant collisions near railway tracks in wildlife-sensitive areas. The system detects the unique vibration patterns produced by elephants as they move, and uses machine learning models to analyze the data. This helps identify elephants and sends alerts to train operators to avoid collisions, thus protecting both wildlife and infrastructure. To gather real-world data, our team visited the Wildlife Department in Colombo, where we met with experts, reviewed incident records, and collected seismic footprint data. This invaluable fieldwork played a crucial role in refining our system’s accuracy and ensuring its practical application for preventing train-elephant collisions in high-risk areas.
